Multimodal Mathematical Reasoning: Frontiers in Integrating Vision, Language, and Symbolic Representations

CVPR 2025 Tutorial

Overview

Welcome to the CVPR 2025 Tutorial on Multimodal Mathematical Reasoning! Artificial intelligence is rapidly evolving toward systems capable of solving complex reasoning tasks across multiple modalities. At the intersection of computer vision, natural language processing, and symbolic computation, multimodal mathematical reasoning has emerged as a critical research frontier. This tutorial brings together cutting-edge developments that enable AI systems to interpret diagrams, process math word problems, and reason over symbolic structures in a unified framework. We will provide an in-depth overview of recent advances, including benchmark construction, symbolic reasoning modules, pretraining and fine-tuning strategies tailored for math tasks, and optimization via reinforcement learning and GRPO. We also explore inference-time scaling and tool-augmented reasoning that push the boundaries of current models. Throughout the tutorial, we aim to identify core challenges and inspire new directions for building interpretable, high-precision, and general-purpose AI reasoning systems.
