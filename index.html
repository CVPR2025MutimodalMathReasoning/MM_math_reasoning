Multimodal Mathematical Reasoning

CVPR 2025 | June 12 | 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM CDT | Nashville, TN

Overview

Mathematical reasoning has long been a cornerstone of scientific discovery and education. Yet, extending modern AI systems to perform robust and interpretable reasoning across multimodal inputs—images, language, and symbolic expressions—remains a significant challenge.

This tutorial presents the frontiers of multimodal mathematical reasoning, a rapidly emerging area at the intersection of computer vision, natural language processing, and symbolic AI. Recent advances in vision-language models have shown promise in tasks like diagram understanding, math problem-solving, and visual question answering. However, integrating these modalities for precise, multi-step mathematical reasoning requires new model architectures, training strategies, and evaluation methods.

Join us as we chart a path toward building general-purpose, explainable, and high-precision reasoning systems that combine the best of visual, linguistic, and symbolic intelligence!
